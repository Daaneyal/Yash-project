CLEAN WATER AND SANITATION PROJECT

Project to help with the need of clean water

BILLIONS OF PEOPLE LACK ACCESS TO CLEAN HYGENIC WATER

WHICH IS THE MOST BASIC NEED FOR HUMAN HEALTH AND WELLBEING.

THIS PROJECT WILL AIM TO HELP THESE ISSUES

OUR AIMS

WHATS THE GOAL?

THE GOAL IS TO HELP PROVIDE CLEAN WATER AND SANITATION. TO ENSURE ACCESS TO SAFE WATER SOURCES AND SANITAIION FOR EVERY SINGLE PERSON ACROSS THE WORLD.

WHY?

EVERYONE SHOULD HAVE ACCESS TO CLEAN HYGENIC WATER AND SANITATION. ITS A BASIC HUMAN RIGHT THAT UNFORTUNATELY BILLIONS ARE UNABLE TO GAIN ACCESS TO. WATER IS ESSENTIAL WHEN IT COMES TO HUMAN HEALTH AND EVEN POVERTY REDUCTION, FOOD SECURITY, ECOSYSTEMS AND EDUCATION.

WHAT ARE THE CHALLENGES?

IN THE YEAR 2020, 4.2 BILLION PEOPLE WERE STILL FACING ISSUES ON A DAILY BASIS IN TERMS OF ACCESS TO CLEAN WATER AND SANITATION. WASHING HANDS WITH CLEAN WATER IS ONE OF THE MOST CHEAPEST, EFFECTIVE AND EASIEST WAYS TO STOP THE SPREAD OF BACTERIA AND DISEASES LIKE CORONAVIRUS.

IN 2016, ONE IN FOUR FACILITIES AROUND THE WORLD LACKED WATER, SANIATION AND HYGIENE SERVICES. ONE IN FIVE HAD NO SANITATION SERVICES AND TWO IN FIVE HAD NO SOAP AND WATER OR ALCOHOL-BASED HAND DETERGENT.

WHAT CAN WE DO?


BY 2030, WE AIM TO ACHIEVE UNIVERSAL AND AND EQUITABLE ACCESS TO SAFE AND AFFORDABLE DRINKING WATER FOR ALL.

BY 2030, ACHIEVE ACCESS TO ADEQUATE AND EQUITABLE SANITATION AND HYGIENE FOR ALL AND END OPEN DEFACTION, PAYING SPECIAL ATTENTION TO THE NEEDS OF WOMEN AND GIRLS AND THOSE IN VULNERABLE SITUATIONS.

BY 2030, IMPROVE WATER QUALITY BY REDUCING POLLUTION, ELIMINATING DUMPING AND MINIMIZING RELEASE OF HAZARDOUS CHEMICALS AND MATERIALS, HALVING THE PROPORTION OF UNTREATED WASTEWATER AND SUBSTANTIALLY INCREASING RECYCLING AND SAFE REUSE GLOBALLY.

BY 2030, SUBSTANTIALLY INCREASE WATER-USE EFFICIENCY ACROSS ALL SECTORS AND ENSURE SUSTAINABLE WITHDRAWALS AND SUPPLY OF FRESHWATER TO ADDRESS WATER SCARCITY AND SUBSTANTIALLY REDUCE THE NUMBER OF PEOPLE SUFFERING FROM WATER SCARCITY.

BY 2030, IMPLEMENT INTEGRATED WATER RESOURCES MANAGEMENT AT ALL LEVELS, INCLUDING THROUGH TRANSBOUNDARY COOPERATION AS APPROPIATE.

BY 2030, WE AIM TO ACHIEVE UNIVERSAL AND AND EQUITABLE ACCESS TO SAFE AND AFFORDABLE DRINKING WATER FOR ALL.
